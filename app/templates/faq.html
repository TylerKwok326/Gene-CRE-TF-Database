Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using the database

General Questions

What types of data can I extract from this database?

Our databae provides the following data types:

  • Gene expression profiles across conditions
  • CRE genomic coordinates and activity states
  • TF binding interactions and networks
  • Pathway associations for genes
  • Disease-specific regulatory relationships
How current is the data in this database?

We maintain our database through regular updates:

  • Quarterly updates from published studies
  • New ATAC-seq/CUT&Tag data from collaborators
  • Monthly TF interaction updates from STRING DB
Can I search using multiple conditions or cell types at once?

Unfortunately, no. You'll have to conduct search in one at a time. The current database only supports one condition and one cell type per query.

What are some limitations of the database?

Some limitation our database faces:

  • Limited conditions
  • Limited cell type
  • Ability to incorportate external data
Where can I report error or suggest improvements?

Your feedbacks are valuable to us. If you spot any errors or have suggestions, please find our contact details in the "About US" page, under the "Contact Our Team" tab.

Technical Questions/ Troubleshooting

How are CRE-gene interactions determined?

We use three complementary methods:

  1. Physical proximity (within 500kb of TSS)
  2. Hi-C chromatin interaction data
  3. TF-mediated co-regulation evidence
Which experimental conditions are available for each cell type?

The database currently includes the following cell types and conditions:

iPSC Microglia
  • IFN treatment
  • TREM2 R47H mutation
  • TREM2 knockout (KO)
ESC Microglia
  • IFN treatment
  • Xenotransplantation (7 days)
  • Coculture assay
Why am I only getting the selected condition and cell type in the results?

Ensure you have selected at least one option in the "Include in Results" field.

What do I do if the website is slow during a large query?

You may shorten wait times by appling additional search parameters to narrow down your query.

How do I handle ambiguous gene symbols (e.g, "MALAT1" vs. "NEAT1")?

The database accepts Gene Symbol, Entrez ID, or Ensembl ID. We recommend using Entrez or Ensembl IDs to avoid ambiguity.
